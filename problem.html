The "And Then" Trap

Most first drafts sound like this:

"I joined the robotics team and then we started building the robot and then we competed and then we won the regional championship and then I learned the value of teamwork."

This is chronology, not reflection. You're telling me what happened in order, but you're not pausing to think about what it meant. The story moves forward, but it never goes deeper.

The Fix

You don't owe the reader the full story. You owe them your thinking.

You need to interrupt the story to examine what's beneath the surface.

Think of it like this:

  • Chronology = "This happened, then this happened, then this happened."
  • Reflection = "Stop the tape. Why did that specific moment surprise me? What was I assuming before it happened? What does it reveal about how I see the world?"
