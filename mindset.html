The College Essay Is Different.

It's not about what you've learned. It's about who you are.

Academic essays have clear rubrics and established structures. They test what you've learned.

The college essay asks you to do something harder: look at your own life and figure out what you actually want to say about it.

This isn't a greatest hits reel. It's not the saddest story you can think of. It's not what you think admissions officers want to hear. It's about discovery-writing to uncover your thoughts, not just reporting what you already know.

"Between the grades, the activities, and the accomplishments on your transcript, who are you?"

Before you can answer that, you need to find your material. The students who write the strongest essays spend time here-exploring their experiences, finding moments that matter, and drafting to discover what they actually have to say.

Don't skip this step.
