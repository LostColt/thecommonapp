The Six Moves

Specific tools to interrupt the narrative and force reflection.

These are specific tools to interrupt the narrative and force reflection.

1. Interrupt Move

Action: Pause to ask the question the moment raises.

Example: "Why did this matter more than I expected?"

2. Then/Now Move

Action: Contrast what you thought then with what you understand now.

Example: "Back then I thought X. Now I see it differently."

3. Zoom-Out Move

Action: Connect the moment to a larger pattern in your life.

Example: "This wasn't the first time I'd..."

4. Cost Move

Action: Name what you gained AND what you lost.

Example: "I earned X. I also lost Y."

5. Contradiction Move

Action: Hold two conflicting truths at once.

Example: "I felt proud. I also felt uncomfortable."

6. Sensory-Anchor Move

Action: Return to a concrete detail that carries meaning.

Example: Using a specific sound, image, or sensation as a touchstone.
