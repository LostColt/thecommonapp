The Field Guide to Pitfalls

     

     

Opening Mistakes

  • Throat-Clearing: "I have always been the kind of person who..." (Wasting words on setup).
  • Generic Opening: "I have always been interested in science." (Could apply to anyone).
  • The Fix: Start late, grab early. Jump into a specific moment within the first sentence.

Structure Mistakes

  • Resume Format: Listing accomplishments ("I organized five fundraisers...") instead of telling a story.
  • Covering Too Much Ground: Trying to fit your whole life into 650 words.
  • The Fix: One moment. One insight. Go deep.

Content Mistakes

  • Tell, Don't Show: "I am a hard worker." (Show us the work instead).
  • Generic Praise: Praising the school's "reputation" without specifics.
  • Trauma Dump: Focusing 100% on the hardship, 0% on the growth.
  • Humble Bragging: "Everyone comes to me for advice."

Voice Mistakes

  • Resume Language: Using words like "facilitated," "leveraged," "endeavored."
  • Thesaurus Syndrome: Using "plethora" or "myriad" to sound smart.
  • The Fix: Use the Coffee Shop Test. Write like you talk to a friend.

Reflection Mistakes

  • Stating the Lesson: "I learned the importance of hard work." (Show how you figured it out).
  • Missing "So What?": Describing events without explaining why they matter.
  • Fake Insight: "I learned to never give up." (Generic platitude).

Ending Mistakes

  • Restating Everything: Summarizing what you just said.
  • "I'm Ready for College": "This is why I'm prepared for the challenges..." (Don't say this).
  • Trailing Off: Ending without closure.

Revision Mistakes

  • Not Reading Aloud: Missing rhythm and flow issues.
  • Over-Editing: Revising until the voice is sterile.
  • Not Using Feedback: Ignoring advice to improve.
