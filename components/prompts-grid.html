Prompt The Goal of the Prompt Focus Area / Key Insight
1. Background, Identity, or Interest

Share a meaningful aspect of your identity (background, culture, or passion) that makes your application incomplete without it.

"Some students have a background, identity, interest, or talent... please share your story."

Perspective in Action: Show *how* this element has shaped your worldview or led you to make specific decisions. Focus on **anecdotes**, not summaries.
2. Challenge, Setback, or Failure

Recount a time when you faced a challenge, setback, or failure. How did it affect you, and what did you learn?

"The lessons we take from obstacles... what did you learn from the experience?"

Maturity & Recovery: Admissions is interested in the **recovery process** and the **internal reflection**, not the initial event. Dedicate most of the essay to the lesson, not the struggle.
3. Questioning a Belief or Idea

Reflect on a time when you questioned or challenged a belief or idea. What prompted your thinking? What was the outcome?

"Reflect on a time when you questioned or challenged a belief or idea..."

Intellectual Evolution: Demonstrate **independent thought** and a capacity for nuance. The best essays show the *process* of changing your mind or learning to hold two complex ideas at once.
4. Gratitude

Reflect on something that someone has done for you that has made you happy or thankful in a surprising way.

"Reflect on something that someone has done for you that has made you happy..."

Empathy & Values: Use the act of kindness as a springboard to discuss your own deep-seated values. This essay is ultimately about **your character**, shown through your reaction to another's act.
5. Accomplishment, Event, or Realization

Discuss an accomplishment, event, or realization that sparked a period of personal growth and a new understanding of yourself or others.

"Discuss an accomplishment, event, or realization that sparked a period of personal growth..."

The "Aha!" Moment: Ignore the accomplishment itself. Focus intensely on the **"realization"**—the fundamental change in your understanding that occurred *after* the event.

6. Engaging Topic, Idea, or Concept

Describe a topic, idea, or concept you find so engaging that it makes you lose all track of time. Why does it captivate you?

"Describe a topic, idea, or concept you find so engaging that it makes you lose all track of time."

Curiosity & Drive: A pure measure of **intellectual curiosity.** Show your unprompted drive to learn and explore, which demonstrates your potential as a thriving college student.
7. Topic of Your Choice

Share an essay on any topic of your choice. It can be one you've already written, one that responds to a different prompt, or one of your own design.

"Share an essay on any topic of your choice..."

Unique Revelation: Only use this if your story absolutely cannot fit into the other six. The essay must be undeniably strong and reveal something about you that is **missing from the rest of your application.**
⭐

Key Takeaway: Think of the essay prompts not as topics, but as **lenses** through which you reveal your internal process. Choose the lens that gives the most intimate view of your self-awareness.
